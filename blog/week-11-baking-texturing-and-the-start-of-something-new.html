The next question is whether to rig and animate characters in Maya or Houdini. Normally rigging and animating are done in Maya, however, Houdini is a huge powerhouse of a toolset that is awaiting some animation exploration, and this seems like the perfect opportunity for it. Below are the pros and cons of animating in each Digital Content Creation (DCC) package.

Maya

Pros

  • Familiar
  • Work on Matrix math/rigging
  • New opensource rigging system for artists

Cons

  • Overdone/saturated market

Maybes

  • This project is more about trying new things, and I have been using Maya for over twelve years now.

Houdini

Pros

  • Proceduralism? Not sure how this would work with animation.
  • New things to try out. Brain will grow.

Cons

  • Houdini could be resilient since few people author character animations with it.
  • More time would be spent on building a rigging system and getting familiar with animation

Maybes

  • Could maybe opensource a rigging system too; however, it's likely to be harder or maybe even unnecessary.

After much thought and consideration, I have decided to rig and animate in Maya, for now... As much as I want to rig and animate in Houdini to try something new one, I am already whelmed with the project given my internal deadline of one year. So, Houdini's rigging and animating will have to wait for another project.

With that being said, I have started the GitHub repo for the rigging and pipe system I will be using, if you want to follow, collaborate, or use it for your project, feel free to peek a look here
